Commercial content moderators are employed by third-party companies as independent contractors rather than IT companies directly. This arrangement results in severe underpayment, with many moderators earning roughly $8 per hour, and geographic dispersion around the world. As a result, these workers are forced to reconcile the discrepancies between their own cultural, linguistic, contextual, and political understanding of the content they are reviewing and the primarily Western lens that represents the companies they are moderating for.
